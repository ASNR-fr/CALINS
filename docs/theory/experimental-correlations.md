# Reflections on Experimental Correlations

If several experiments intrinsically share very similar sensitivities and responses (both experimental and calculated)—for instance, experiments from the same series—then neglecting correlations biases the assimilation process. This appears to result in an overestimation of posterior uncertainty and an inaccurate evaluation of the associated bias.

Studies on the impact of experimental correlations have already been carried out, notably [^1].
